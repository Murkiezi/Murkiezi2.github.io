Case Study: Sophia Marie Coquilla

One of the most recent and tragic cases is the murder of Sophia Marie Coquilla, a 19-year-old student from Tagum City. She was found dead in her bedroom, suspected to have been killed during a robbery. Police investigations revealed that minors were involved in the crime. Due to current laws, these suspects cannot be punished as severely as adults.

Other Examples

Across the Philippines, more cases of minors being involved in robbery, assault, and drug-related crimes have been reported. These highlight the urgent need to reconsider the age of criminal liability.

Internationally, countries like Singapore and the United Kingdom hold minors accountable at much younger ages, showing a different approach to juvenile justice.
